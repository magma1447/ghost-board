# Cricket

Close **15, 16, 17, 18, 19, 20 and the bull** — hit each three times — then score on your closed numbers to win.

## How to play

- A single = 1 mark, a double = 2 marks, a treble = 3. Outer bull = 1 mark, inner bull (50) = 2.
- Hitting a number three times **closes** it for you (shown as a filled ●).
- Once you've closed a number, extra hits **score** its value (bull = 25) — but only while an opponent still hasn't closed it. A number closed by everyone is dead.
- **Win** by closing all seven numbers with a points total at least as high as every opponent.

## Options

- **Scoring**:
  - **Standard** — you keep the points; **highest** total among those who've closed out wins.
  - **Cut-throat** — your points are handed to every opponent who hasn't closed that number, so the **lowest** total wins. Points become a weapon.
  - **Simple (no score)** — no points at all; first to close all seven numbers wins. The gentlest way to learn Cricket.
- **Numbers** — **15–20 + bull** (standard), or **Random**, drawn fresh each game to level the field — *w/ bull* keeps the bull as the seventh target, *w/o bull* draws seven numbers instead.

# X01

Count down from a starting score to exactly **zero**. The first player to reach zero wins.

## How to play

- Each player starts on the chosen score (301, 501, …).
- On your turn you throw up to three darts; each dart's value is subtracted from your score.
- Singles score their number, doubles double it, trebles treble it. The outer bull scores 25, the inner bull 50.
- Reaching exactly zero wins. Going below zero — or to a score you can't finish from — is a **bust**: your score reverts to what it was at the start of the turn.

## Options

- **Starting score** — the score each player counts down from.
- **Double in** — until you hit a double, your darts don't count; the double that "opens" you is the first to score.
- **Double out** — the dart that takes you to zero must be a double (the bull counts). Landing on 1 is a bust, since you can't finish from it.
- **Bull scoring** — whether the outer bull is worth 25 (standard) or 50.
- **Max rounds** — if no one has finished after this many rounds, the game ends in a draw.
- **Checkout calls below** — when your score drops to this threshold or lower, checkout darts are announced.
